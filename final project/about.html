A Journey Through Time

1937 - The Beginning

Edwin H. Land establishes Polaroid Corporation in Cambridge, Massachusetts, initially focusing on polarized sunglasses and optical filters. His curiosity about light and imaging sets the stage for a photography revolution.

1937

1948 - Instant Photography is Born

Land unveils the Polaroid Land Camera Model 95 at a department store in Boston. Using self-developing film, it produces a finished photo in just 60 secondsâ€”a world first that captivates the public.

1948

1972 - The SX-70 Era

Polaroid launches the SX-70, a foldable, single-lens reflex camera with instant color prints. Hailed as a design masterpiece, it becomes a cultural icon, embraced by artists like Andy Warhol.

1972

1980s - The 600 Series Boom

The Polaroid 600 series introduces high-speed film and built-in flash, making instant photography more accessible. Models like the Sun 660 become staples at parties and family gatherings worldwide.

1980s

2001-2008 - Challenges & Revival

Facing the rise of digital photography, Polaroid files for bankruptcy in 2001. After ceasing film production in 2008, fans and entrepreneurs rally to revive the brand, sparking a resurgence of analog love.

2001-2008

Today - A Modern Legacy

Polaroid thrives again with updated cameras like the Polaroid Now and I-2, blending retro aesthetics with modern tech. The brand remains a symbol of creativity, nostalgia, and instant connection.
